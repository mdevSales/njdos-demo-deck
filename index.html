Prepared for the CIO & HR Head · NJ DOS

Today, the story breaks four times

Every hire at NJ DOS moves through four disconnected systems. Each handoff is a chance to drop information, delay a candidate, or lose a strong hire to another employer.

1

Careers & application intake

Public site, PDF resumes into a shared inbox.

2

Recruiter workspace

Spreadsheets, manual resume screening, side-channel emails.

3

Offer & legal

Word templates, mail merges, IT tickets that get forgotten.

4

Onboarding & HR

Employee re-created in a separate HRIS, tasks re-entered, day one chaos.

What we'll walk through today

A single platform that carries one candidate — Paul Marshall — from a public careers site through his first day as an NJ DOS employee. Same data, same records, four different faces for four different people.

"Related roles" surfaces adjacent openings based on skill overlap. If this job isn't Paul's, the platform tries to keep him in the state's pipeline anyway.

Stage 4 · Triage

Recruiter Workspace

NJ DOS Recruiter
Talent Team

What the recruiter sees

Monday morning: one queue, applications grouped by role, sorted by qualification fit. Top of queue is the highest-probability hire, not the newest application.

Where AI shows up

Every candidate arrives pre-scored against the job requisition. The recruiter's time goes to the top ten, not to sorting the bottom thirty.

Stage 5 · Assessment

Candidate Record with AI Summary

NJ DOS Recruiter
Talent Team

What the recruiter sees

Paul's record with a plain-English AI summary at the top: experience, skill match, and gaps. Fifteen minutes of resume-reading collapses to three seconds of context.

Where AI shows up

Conversational qualification match — the recruiter can ask "which requirements does Paul meet?" and get a per-requirement breakdown, each answer citing the exact line from his resume. Decision support, not decision replacement.

Stage 6 · Progression

Candidate Pipeline

NJ DOS Recruiter
Talent Team

What the recruiter sees

A visible pipeline: Submitted → In Review → Interview → Offer → Accepted → Hired. Moving Paul forward is a single click, and the system knows what to do next.

Automation behind each stage change

Move to In Review? Hiring manager gets the AI summary. Move to Interview? Scheduling kicks in. Move to Offer? Legal review is notified. Every handoff is instrumented.

Stage 7 · Evaluation

Interview Feedback

Hiring Manager
Engineering Lead

What the hiring manager sees

A structured evaluation form: score per interviewer, per competency (technical, communication, mission fit). Everyone measured against the same rubric — comparable, auditable, defensible.

Where AI shows up

Interview synthesis: consensus themes, dissenting concerns, hiring recommendation. Grounds the go/no-go conversation in a shared picture — efficiency and fairness in the same motion.

Stage 8 · Offer

Offer Generation & Delivery

NJ DOS Recruiter
Talent Team

What the recruiter sees

A pre-filled offer generator that appears on Paul's record the moment he reaches "Offer." Name, title, start date, salary — already there. One click renders a branded PDF, one click sends it.

What used to take a week

Word docs, mail merges, chasing legal, forgetting to CC someone. Now: generate, preview, send — one click, tracked, version-controlled on the record.

Stage 9 · Conversion · Hero Moment

Candidate → Employee, in One Click

NJ DOS Recruiter
Talent Team

What the recruiter sees

Paul accepts. A "Convert to Employee" step appears. Three-step guided flow: confirm details (all pre-filled), pick an onboarding playbook (engineering, sales, or standard), review and convert.

The gap that closes to zero

One click creates the Employee record, generates a role-specific onboarding checklist (9 tasks for engineering), and links back to Paul's application. No re-typing. No dropped tasks. One continuous story.

What this means for you

For the CIO

One platform, not four

  • Consolidation: careers, ATS, offer generation, HR onboarding — one data model, one security model, one place to audit.
  • AI grounded in your data: every AI output cites its source; nothing hallucinated, everything reviewable.
  • Extensible: the same platform is already running your policy workflows. Talent joins as another tenant, not another silo.
  • Compliance-friendly: structured evaluations, versioned offer PDFs, full audit trail from application to hire.
For the Head of HR

Recruiters back on human work

  • 15 minutes per resume, reclaimed: AI does the first-pass reading. Your team spends time on candidates, not on triage.
  • Fairness built in: structured evaluations and AI-cited fit summaries reduce interviewer bias and support defensible hiring decisions.
  • Candidate experience: the citizen sees a modern, transparent process — critical for the state's brand as an employer.
  • Day-one ready: zero-gap conversion means laptops, badges, and training are prepped before the new hire walks in.
